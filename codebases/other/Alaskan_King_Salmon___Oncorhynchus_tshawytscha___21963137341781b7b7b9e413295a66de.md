# Alaskan King Salmon (*Oncorhynchus tshawytscha*)

Category: Fish
Origin: Native to the cold, nutrient-rich waters of Alaska, thriving in deep, clean, and cold ocean environments with a high salinity
Health Benefits: Rich in omega-3 fatty acids, supports heart health, boosts brain function, promotes skin health, and provides anti-inflammatory benefits. Known for its rich, buttery flavor and tender texture, commonly used in Alaskan cuisine and fine dining dishes.
Nutritional Profile: High in omega-3 fatty acids, protein, vitamins B12 and D, selenium, and essential minerals like potassium. Contains bioactive compounds that help improve cardiovascular health, enhance cognitive function, and protect skin from oxidative stress.
Preparation & Dosage: Traditionally consumed grilled, roasted, or pan-seared, or used in sushi, salads, and soups. To use, consume 1–2 servings (150–200 grams) of cooked king salmon daily or add to dishes like pasta, salads, or seafood platters. Dosage: 1 serving daily for heart, brain, and skin health support.

Historical and Cultural Context: Alaskan King Salmon has been a prized fish in Alaskan culture for centuries, valued for its health benefits, particularly for cardiovascular health, brain function, and skin protection.
